# Analysis
a = cali %>% 
  split(.$experiment) %>% 
  map(~difference_in_means(listA ~ trt_A, data = .)) %>% 
  map(tidy) %>% 
  bind_rows(.id = "experiment")

b = cali %>% 
  split(.$experiment) %>% 
  map(~difference_in_means(listB ~ trt_B, data = .)) %>% 
  map(tidy) %>% 
  bind_rows(.id = "experiment")

pool = cali %>% 
  mutate(id = row_number()) %>% 
  pivot_longer(cols = c(listA, listB)) %>% 
  rename(list = name, count = value) %>% 
  mutate(Z = ifelse(trt_A == 1 & list == "listA" | trt_B == 1 & list == "listB",
                    1, 0)) %>% 
  split(.$experiment) %>% 
  map(~ lm_robust(count ~ Z + list, clusters = id, se_type = "stata", data = .)) %>% 
  map(tidy) %>% 
  bind_rows(.id = "experiment") %>% 
  filter(term == "Z")
  
est_df = rbind(a, b, pool)

est_df$term = fct_relevel(est_df$term, "trt_A", "trt_B", "Z")

Include within this presentation brief instructions to students for a class activity designed to support student learning about this concept (you do not need to implement this activity during your presentation).

Follow this presentation with an outline of how your current and future research will complement and enhance research in the School of Social and Political Sciences at the University of Melbourne.

Total time for presentation: 25 minutes (approximately 20 minutes for the teaching presentation, and 5 minutes for the research overview). Audience members will include the selection panel, and current staff and PhD students in the School. There will be 10 minutes for questions from the audience.

Prevalence rate

\[ \text{Prevalence(X)} = \text{Mean(List with X)} - \text{Mean(List without X)} \]

  • Proportion of individuals in the target population who hold the sensitive attitude or behavior

  • But we do not know who they are in our survey!

List experiments are not always an improvement

Can we do better?

Double list experiment

List A

  • Californians for Disability (advocating for people with disabilities)
  • California National Organization for Women (advocating for women’s equality and empowerment)
  • American Family Association (advocating for pro-family values)
  • American Red Cross (humanitarian organization)

List B

  • American Legion (veterans service organization)
  • Equality California (gay and lesbian advocacy organization)
  • Tea Party Patriots (conservative group supporting lower taxes and limited government)
  • Salvation Army (charitable organization)

Source: Alvarez et al (2019)

Sensitive item

Organization X (advocating for immigration reduction and measures against undocumented immigration)

  • Randomly appears in list A or B

  • Equivalent to making two parallel list experiments

  • Calculate prevalence by averaging the two single-list estimates

But this is not for free

  1. Simulate a survey of \(N = 1000\) that asks a sensitive question

  2. Pick a true prevalence rate

  3. Is the sensitivity bias reduction worth the precision loss?

  4. Would a DLE perform better?

  5. What if the extra piloting of the DLE means you only have a budget for \(N/2\)?

  6. Do your conclusions change if you modify \(N\) or the prevalence rate?
